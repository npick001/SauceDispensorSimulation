
HARDWARE ASSUMPTIONS:
------------------------
Conveyor and Carousel move at consistent speeds.
Dispensing heads do not have a dispensing time (instant sauce dispensing).
Dispensing head is 'aware' of what Ingredient it is dispensing and the amount of said Ingredient.
Dispensing heads will dispense *exactly* the same amount of an Ingredient, every time.
Simulated hardware failures are not supported.

MODEL IMPLEMENTATION ASSUMPTIONS:
------------------------
Model is based on a Discrete-Event methodology.
A Simulation Executive will execute Event Actions and drive simulation time.

The Carousel will be locked with 2 dispensing heads.
Carousel Ingredient cartridges will not move. The movement will be simulated using rotating Ingredient pointers, which the dispensors will be updated with.
To allow for every bowl to be fulfilled, the taskrate of the Conveyor should be taskrate = (speed of carousel) * (number of ingredients)
The Conveyor will manage the queue of orders.
Ingredients will be dispensed with exactly the same amount.
Each ingredient cartridge will have the exact same capacity of an Ingredient.
Bowls will require [0, 2] distinct and nonstacking Ingredients (ex: a bowl will not ask for double Ranch)
Dispensing heads will "try" to dispense every ingredient, but will not succeed. (My assumption is some vision model will say 'hey don't do that!')

Ingredients are limited to: [Ranch, BBQ, Mayo, Hot Sauce, Mustard, Teriyaki, Caeser, Buffalo, Chipotle, Vinaigrette]

Semi-reasonable variable values are:
	- carousel_taskrate = 50 ms
	- conveyor_taskrate = 500 ms
	- dispensor_taskrate = 25 ms
	- bowl_weight_disribution = Triangular distribution [1.0, 2.0, 3.0]
	- (Ingredient) amount_to_dispense = 0.2
	- (Ingredient) capacity = 10
